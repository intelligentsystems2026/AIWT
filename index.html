International Workshop on Agentic AI Workflows and Technologies

AAWT 2026 focuses on the design, engineering, evaluation, and deployment of agentic AI workflows and technologies, including autonomous and semi-autonomous AI agents, multi-agent collaboration, tool-using AI systems, orchestration frameworks, and workflow automation.

The workshop is organized with the 2026 International Conference on Next Generation AI Systems (NGEN-AI 2026), taking place on 1–4 September 2026 at the University of Trento, Italy, and online over Zoom.

Call for Papers

Advancing agentic AI workflows from concepts to dependable systems

AAWT 2026 invites original submissions on the methods, architectures, platforms, tools, and practices needed to build and evaluate agentic AI workflows and technologies. The workshop welcomes research and practice on systems where AI agents plan, reason, call tools, coordinate tasks, interact with humans, access external services, and execute complex workflows across software, data, business, scientific, and industrial environments.

Agentic AI is rapidly changing the way digital work is organized and automated. New AI agents can support information seeking, software development, decision support, data analysis, customer service, operations, research, and many other tasks. However, building agentic systems that are reliable, transparent, secure, controllable, and useful in real settings remains a major challenge.

AAWT focuses on the full lifecycle of agentic workflows, from design and modelling to orchestration, monitoring, evaluation, deployment, governance, and maintenance. The workshop is interested in both single-agent and multi-agent systems, human-in-the-loop workflows, agent communication protocols, tool integration, memory and context management, workflow verification, and operational risks.

The workshop welcomes full research papers, short papers, position papers, experience reports, industrial case studies, tool demonstrations, benchmark papers, datasets, and papers describing lessons learned from real-world agentic AI deployments.

Topics of Interest

Relevant topics include, but are not limited to

Architectures and design patterns for agentic AI systems Agentic workflow modelling, orchestration, and execution
Single-agent and multi-agent systems Tool-using AI systems and integration with external services
Planning, reasoning, memory, and context management for agents Human-in-the-loop and human-on-the-loop agentic workflows
Agent communication, coordination, negotiation, and collaboration Evaluation, benchmarking, and testing of agentic AI workflows
Reliability, robustness, monitoring, and observability of AI agents Security, privacy, safety, and misuse prevention in agentic AI systems
Governance, accountability, explainability, and auditability of agentic workflows LLMOps, AgentOps, DevOps, and lifecycle management for agentic systems
Agentic AI for software engineering, data analysis, operations, and business processes Industrial case studies and real-world deployment lessons
Datasets, benchmarks, tools, platforms, and reproducibility for agentic AI Ethical, organizational, and societal implications of agentic automation
